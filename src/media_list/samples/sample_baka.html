Description
In a world entirely ruled by giants, the human race, which has turned into their food, has surrounded its residential zones with immense walls, which both prevent their freedom outside the walls and protect them from incursions. However, as a result of the appearance of supergiants who cross the wall, a desperate struggle begins and the young heroes, who have lost their parents, fight the giant as a training corps, with a view to regaining their freedom.

Good plot development. Meh art that gets better. Deaths lack impact/meaning.  
by sakirol
May 9th, 2016, 10:37pm
Rating: 9.0 / 10.0
TBH, I dropped Attack on Titan once. I didn't like it in the beginning. People died faster than I could properly recognize their characters, especially with the artwork. I expected the MC to grow stronger and kick ass, but that never happened. He was mostly just a raging ball of a fury, and I didn't really care for his feelings because the deaths happened so fast.

Then I watched the anime, which introduced the characters just enough for it to hit you in the gut when they die and accompanied it with great OST. This is probably one of the few series where I prefer the anime over the manga.

Then I continued onward with the manga, now with a fresh emotional attachment to characters. The deaths still feel emotionally lackluster in the manga, but I think the plot is very solid. Typically once a manga gets to the 'conspiracy revealed' phase, the series deteriorates really fast in terms of story. Plot elements become outlandish and unfit for what the original story was supposed to be about. The mangaka reeled it in right before it got to that point and connected it to rest of the story, so I appreciate it. Just in general, the story is very intact with all the politics and history.

I Like How Isayama Writes Tragedy...  
by Polka-Dotted-Yoshi
February 18th, 2016, 5:03pm
Rating: 8.0 / 10.0
...but not his characters. (I've followed this series since it was made into an anime but I think now's as good a time as any to leave a review for the manga.)

I'll admit, this series has gotten me a bit emotional (particularly the prettied-up anime adaptation) but as I read more and more into the series, I'm surprised I don't feel any attachment towards any of the characters. There are only a couple of characters I can name that received actual, note-worthy development and I won't name them for the sake of spoilers.

As I said before though, Isayama sure can write tragedy. It's realistic how horrified the people are by the idea of titans breaking through the walls and just destroying their homes and lives. However, I think I've shown more of an emotional reaction to the dying background characters than to the main cast and that's actually fairly disappointing in the long run.

This series is a diamond in the rough in the shounen genre for me. The lack of character development just makes me want to scream because of how much I love the horror-based tragic element the series carries.
